Japan

"The land of beautiful nature and efficiency, Japan. It is a string of islands or archipelago in the eastern edge of Asia. You can encounter interesting things by exploring Japan by yourself, where you don't need a tour guide. Japan has a lot of photogenic places, unique temples, and cultures. Whether you like anime or just want to see Japan's beauty with your own eyes, it is one of the best countries to visit when you go alone."


  • Top 3 Recommendation of Places: Japan

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Fancy a bamboo?
How about a whole lot of bamboo?
Well, if you like bamboos, you can go to Arashiyama Bamboo Grove located in Kyoto. You can take the train from Kyoto Station straight to Saga-Arashiyama Station.
This is a place where you can walk along a seemingly endless grove of bamboos. There are japanese-style rickshaws for you to rent at the entrance of the grove and on the inside. The bamboo grove is also a popular spot for taking pictures as the bamboos look ethereal and great for photoshoots.

Gala Yuzawa Snow Resort

Do you like snow?
Well, how about going to this Snow Resort and experience all the snow you want?
Gala Yuzawa Snow Resort is a terrific place to go! You can trek in the snow with a group, go snowboarding and skiing or learn how to, and sled across the vast snow-covered mountains. You can take the Joetsu Shinkansen from Tokyo Station straight to Gala Yuzawa Snow Resort.

Nara Park

If you don't like snow or bamboos, how about deers?
Nara Park is a place where deers roam free. The vast area covers 660 hectares and there are world famous temples nearby.
You can feed the deers, take photos with the deers, and explore the temple nearby. This is a place where you can just relax with deers around the area. It is a 5 to 6 minute walk from the Kintetsu Nara Station or a 20 minute walk from the JR Nara Station to Nara Park.

Travel tips

When traveling alone, renting places are not that ideal as it is expensive.

An alternative way is to go full backpacking-style to Japan and stay at capsule hotels. Also, vending machines are a quick and easy way to get food or drinks on the go.

In addition to all that, Japan is relatively safe and mindful of others' belongings.

If you forget your items in a train from a previous station, you can inform people in the information center in front of stations or the guards up front.
